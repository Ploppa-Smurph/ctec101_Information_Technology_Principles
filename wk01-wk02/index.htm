Chapter 1.3 Technology and Ethics

key terms:
  • Ethics - personal behavior that builds responsibility and trust. more than avoiding unlawful acts, but also upholding standards.
  • Artificial Intelligence - are of Computer Science that focuses on intelligent machines
  • Intellectual Property - content owned by a copyright holder
  • Dark Web - network of encrypted content that allows untraceable activity
  • Green Computing - enviromentally sound policies that prescribe energy efficiency, recycling, and eco-friendly disposal of equipment
  • Ergonomics - study of how people function most effectively in their work environment
  • Blue Light - range of visible light spectrum, can lead to eyestrain, headache, fatigue, and vision problems
  • Cyberloafing - employee using company internet for personal use on company time
  • Employee Monitoring - observing and recording employee activity, sometimes without their consent
  • Net Neutrality - policy that required ISP to allow equal access to all content
  • Predictive Policing - Law Enforcement practice that involves analyzing surveillance footage, social media, and more to prevent crimes
  • Hacktivism - malicious attack on networks and websites to promote social change, regarded as some as civil-disobedience and others as cyber-terrorism
  • Doxing - publishing private contact information, ie: to punish one's political opponent

Ethical Principles

  • Confidentiality - protect data we are not authorized to share
  • Human Dignity - respecting the inherent worth of other indiviual
  • Civility - observe the rules of 'netiquette' which promote courtesy and respect
  • Digital Property Rights - recognizing and respecting the creations of others
it is important to create and follow a 'Personal Code of Ethics', or set of rules and standards that dictate how you act
  • values - provide the basis for a code of ethics. identify the beliefs and ideals that guide your actions
  • principles - the fundamental truths that support your personal values. focus on honesty, integrity, and respect for others
  • personal responsibility - reinforces adherence to values and principles. ie: how you would act if no one is looking
  • legal compliance - involves obeying laws and regulations.
the ISC organization of Computer Security Professionals 'Code of Ethics' is:
-protect society, the commonwealth, and the infrastructure.
-act honorably, honestly, justly, responsibly, and legally.
-provide diligent and competent service to principals.
-advance and protect the profession.
